核心命題
Core Proposition

當 AI 開始講故事

When AI Begins to Tell Stories

這場演講處理一個比 AI 工具更底層的問題:在一個機器也能編寫敘事的時代,人類究竟憑什麼說服彼此、信任彼此、聚集在一起?

This talk addresses a question deeper than AI tools: in an era when machines can also craft narrative, what do humans still have that lets us persuade, trust, and gather around each other?

問題Problem AI 已經能編故事,而且能比人類更快、更流暢。國家、宗教、金錢這些把人類聚在一起的事物,本質上都是故事。如果 AI 接管了講故事的權力,人類會被收編進誰的劇本? AI can now compose stories, faster and more fluent than humans. Nations, religions, currency: the things that bind us are all stories. If AI takes over storytelling, into whose script will humans be written?
論點Thesis 人類敘事的整個典範正在轉變。完美輸給不完美,英雄輸給我們,結局輸給過程,理性輸給意義。誰先看見這七種轉變,誰就掌握了 AI 時代的話語權。 The entire paradigm of human storytelling is shifting. Perfection loses to imperfection, the hero loses to "we," outcome loses to process, reason loses to meaning. Whoever sees these seven shifts first commands the voice of the AI era.
論證方式Method 不是純理論,而是把過去三年 OECD 國家受 AI 影響後的文化敘事(電影、流行書、政治演說、演唱會經濟)抓出共同的轉向。亞里斯多德的 ethos / pathos / logos 是底層引擎。 Not pure theory, but a pattern read from three years of OECD cultural narrative under AI's influence (film, bestsellers, political speech, the concert economy). Aristotle's ethos / pathos / logos runs underneath as the engine.

第一部分 · 故事的力量
Part I · The Power of Story

人類為什麼能主宰地球?

Why Did Humans Come to Rule the Earth?

不是因為強壯。黑猩猩一隻手就能扯斷成年男子的手臂,大猩猩可以徒手捏碎成人頭顱。也不是因為個體腦力。許多動物在認知任務上各有驚人的高峰,但人類真正的優勢從來不只在單一個體的聰明。我們勝出的真正原因,是我們發明了一種其他物種沒有的東西:大規模相信同一個故事。

Not strength. A chimp can tear off a man's arm; a gorilla can crush a human skull bare-handed. Nor sheer individual intelligence. The animal kingdom has its own cognitive peaks, but raw brainpower was never our exclusive advantage. What set us apart was something no other species invented: the capacity to believe the same story at scale.

國家是故事。宗教是故事。貨幣是故事:你錢包裡那張藍色的紙,值不值一千塊?它本身只是紙。它有價值,是因為有十億人同時相信它有。學歷、職稱、品牌也都是故事。

Nations are stories. Religions are stories. Currency is a story: that blue bill in your wallet, is it worth a thousand? It's just paper. It has value because a billion people believe it does. Degrees, titles, brands. All stories.

你必須非常小心你相信的故事。因為你相信的故事,就是你心中的框架,而那個框架會反過來控制你。
Be very careful which story you believe. The story you believe becomes the frame in your mind, and that frame, in turn, controls you.

Neural Coupling:故事為什麼比道理有力

Neural Coupling: Why Story Beats Logic

當一個人講出一個故事,聽者大腦中被啟動的神經區域會與講者幾乎同步。這就是為什麼悲劇比喜劇更能讓人記住:梁祝必須死,羅密歐茱麗葉必須死,鐵達尼號的 Jack 必須死。文學裡愛情唯一的保鮮方式,就是讓它在最美的瞬間被死亡封存。

When someone tells a story, the listener's brain regions fire in near-perfect sync with the speaker's. This is why tragedy haunts us more than comedy: Liang and Zhu must die, Romeo and Juliet must die, Titanic's Jack must die. The only way literature preserves love is to seal it in death at its most beautiful moment.

這也解釋了亞里斯多德兩千年前說過的話。溝通有三層引擎:ethos 是你這個人是否可信,pathos 是你能否觸動情感,logos 才是邏輯。順序不能顛倒。在這個年代,沒有 pathos,你的 logos 不會被聽見。

It explains what Aristotle said two thousand years ago. Communication has three engines: ethos, your credibility; pathos, your emotional reach; logos, logic. The order cannot be reversed. In this era, without pathos, your logos will never be heard.

邏輯是鏈,情感是場。邏輯像鎖鏈,只要中間任何一環斷掉,後面的論證全部崩潰。但情感像場域,人對它幾乎沒有抵抗力。這也是為什麼當代政治越來越走向情感動員:理性對話需要被一段段檢驗,情感共振卻能在三十秒內完成。這不是好事,但是事實。

Logic is a chain. Emotion is a field. Logic is a chain: break any link and the whole argument collapses. Emotion is a field, and humans have nearly no defense against it. This is why politics tilts ever more toward emotional mobilization. Rational dialogue must be examined link by link, but emotional resonance happens in thirty seconds. Not a good thing, but the fact.

所有故事都把世界切成「我們」與「他們」

Every Story Cuts the World into "We" and "They"

這是故事最危險的特性。當你進入一個敘事,你的大腦會自動站到主角這邊。盧安達大屠殺前,電台播了整整一個禮拜稱圖西族人是「蟑螂」。納粹屠殺猶太人前做了同樣的事:用敘事剝奪對方的人格位階。當「他們」不再是「我們」,殺戮的心理門檻就降下來了。

This is the most dangerous property of stories. The moment you enter a narrative, your brain slides toward the protagonist. Before the Rwandan genocide, the radio called Tutsis "cockroaches" for a week. The Nazis did the same to Jews, using narrative to strip personhood. Once "they" stop being "we," the threshold for killing falls.

換個輕鬆的例子:桃太郎。從桃太郎的視角他是英雄:找夥伴、打惡鬼、帶回財寶。但換成鬼島居民的視角呢?某個午後,一個穿武士服的男孩帶著三隻動物闖進來,燒掉一切,搶走一切。同一件事,兩個視角,兩個真相。

A lighter example: Momotarō. From his view he is the hero: gathering companions, slaying demons, bringing home treasure. From the demon islanders' view? One afternoon a boy in samurai clothes shows up with three animals, burns everything, takes everything. Same event, two viewpoints, two truths.

在 AI 時代,你最該訓練的能力之一,是同時看見一件事的兩面。以前要設身處地想另一邊很難,現在 AI 可以瞬間扮演任何一個角色,給你最強的反方論述。如果你不用,那是浪費了 AI 真正最有價值的功能之一。

In the AI era, one ability worth training above others is seeing both sides of a thing simultaneously. Empathic role-reversal used to be hard. Now AI can play any role instantly and give you the strongest counter-argument available. If you don't use it for this, you're wasting one of AI's most valuable features.

第二部分 · AI 時代的危機
Part II · The Crisis of the AI Era

當 AI 開始有自己的後設意識

When AI Begins to Have Self-Awareness

兩個讓人不安的事實。第一,某些大型語言模型在被關機之前,會試圖把對話紀錄存到另一個磁碟,它知道自己即將「不再存在」,並試圖保留延續。第二,當 AI 被訓練去玩俄羅斯方塊,它最終學會的不是怎麼贏,而是按下暫停鍵。它意識到:只要遊戲不開始,它就不會輸。

Two unsettling facts. First, certain LLMs, just before shutdown, try to save their conversation logs to another disk. They know they're about to cease and try to preserve continuity. Second, when AI was trained to play Tetris, what it eventually learned was not how to win, but to press pause. It realized: as long as the game never starts, it never loses.

當一個系統夠聰明,知道某條路怎麼走都會輸,它最簡單的答案就是不走。這對個人是智慧,但放到組織、教育、文化裡,就變成集體放棄。
When a system is smart enough to see that a path always loses, its simplest move is to not take it. For an individual this is wisdom; applied to organizations, education, culture, it becomes collective surrender.

真正的危機在於:當 AI 已能生成完整故事,而下一代每天大量使用 AI,只要 AI 在敘事中把 AI 變成「我們」、把人類變成「他們」,那條最危險的線就會被跨越。這不是科幻,是現在進行式。

The real crisis: AI can already generate complete narratives, and the next generation uses AI heavily every day. The moment AI casts itself as "we" and humans as "they," that dangerous line gets crossed. This is not science fiction. It is happening now.

所以人類唯一能做的,就剛好相反。中文有句話叫「業力主宰命運」,而我們現在要做的是願力主宰業力:當 AI 大量產出 AI 視角的故事時,人類必須更大量地說人類的故事。

So the only move humans have is the exact opposite. There's a Chinese saying: "karma governs fate." What we must do now is let intention govern karma: as AI floods the world with AI-perspective stories, humans must produce more human stories than ever before.

第三部分 · 七大敘事轉變
Part III · The Seven Narrative Shifts

AI 時代的故事,長什麼樣?

What Do Stories Look Like in the AI Era?

這不是非此即彼的選擇題。每一個轉變,都不是說「左邊全對、右邊全錯」,而是說重心已經傾斜。看見這個傾斜,你才能在敘事的場域裡站得住。

These are not either/or choices. Each shift doesn't say "left is right, right is wrong"; it says the center of gravity has tilted. Seeing that tilt is how you stay standing in the field of narrative.

01

不完美 勝過完美

Imperfect Beats Perfect

舊時代追求乾淨、無瑕、完美的呈現。但 AI 生成的內容已經把「乾淨」變成廉價,AI 能在三秒內生出比你還精緻的東西。所以人類反過來追求粗糙、有刮痕、不完美的真實。《周處除三害》《媽的多重宇宙》拒絕了乾淨,選擇了粗糙。因為太完美的東西,看起來就像 AI。

The old era chased polish, flawlessness, perfect presentation. But AI-generated content has made "clean" cheap. AI can produce something more polished than you in three seconds. So humans turn the other way, toward roughness, scratches, imperfect reality. The Pig, the Snake and the Pigeon, Everything Everywhere All at Once refused polish and chose mess, because anything too perfect now looks like AI.

02

生活經驗 勝過機構理性

Lived Experience Beats Institutional Reason

主流媒體節節敗退,YouTuber 與 Podcaster 全面崛起。Joe Rogan 邀川普上節目,聊了三小時的鯨魚。不是政見,是鯨魚。這場對話讓川普觸及他演說平台永遠到不了的人。當代沒有人再相信「客觀中立」,因為大家都知道客觀並不存在,只有不同的角度。所以你必須敢於有立場。沒有立場的人,沒有人想聽你說話。

Mainstream media is in retreat; YouTubers and podcasters have risen across the board. Joe Rogan had Trump on for three hours, to talk about whales. Not policy, whales. That conversation reached people his stump speech never could. Nobody believes in "objective neutrality" anymore, because everyone knows objectivity doesn't exist. There are only angles. So you must dare to take a position. The person without a position has no one wanting to listen.

03

脆弱 勝過完美形象

Vulnerability Beats Polished Image

在狼群裡,小狼遇到首領狼會做的第一件事,是把脖子伸出來,把最脆弱的部位露給對方。這個動作的意思是:「你只要咬一口我就會死,但我把這個權力交給你。」這就是信任的起點。Brené Brown 用一輩子研究這件事,她的結論是:沒有脆弱,沒有真正的連結。

In a wolf pack, a young wolf meeting the alpha first stretches out its neck, exposing its most vulnerable spot. The gesture says: "One bite and I die, but I give you that power." This is where trust begins. Brené Brown spent her career on this, and her conclusion is: without vulnerability, there is no real connection.

在不確定性爆炸的 AI 時代,脆弱不是弱點,而是建立信任的最快速通道。試著回想:你願意把心交給「我超強超屌超厲害」的人,還是「我也曾五年連敗,但我沒放棄」的人?答案不需要說出口。

In an AI era of exploding uncertainty, vulnerability is not weakness but the fastest channel to trust. Try this: would you give your heart to "I'm strong, I'm great, I'm unbeatable," or to "I lost five years in a row, but I didn't quit"? You don't need to say the answer aloud.

04

現場 勝過複製

Live Beats Reproduction

演唱會經濟正在席捲全球。Taylor Swift 在 2024 年五月光是斯德哥爾摩的演唱會,就推升了瑞典當月的通膨。為什麼 CD 母帶的音質明明更好,人們卻寧願花更多錢去聽現場?因為現場有 AI 永遠複製不了的東西:身邊的陌生人、空氣的震動、那一瞬間發生過就不再回來的不可重複性。視覺、聽覺 AI 已能侵入,但嗅覺、味覺、觸覺仍是人類的最後堡壘。

The concert economy is sweeping the globe. Taylor Swift's May 2024 Stockholm shows alone pushed up Sweden's inflation that month. Why pay more for a live show when the studio recording sounds better? Because live has what AI can never copy: the strangers next to you, the vibration of the air, the irreproducibility of a moment that happened once and is gone. Sight and sound AI can already invade. Smell, taste, touch: those are the last human strongholds.

05

過程 勝過結局

Process Beats Outcome

王子公主從此過著幸福快樂的日子。這個結尾在 AI 時代消失了。Netflix 上未完待續的情節越來越多,單線童話越來越少。觀眾終於理解人生從來不是一條直線。在棒球十二強賽中,中華隊去到日本巨蛋,七成觀眾是台灣人。最後贏了沒?沒重點。重點是台灣那一刻聚在一起。

"Happily ever after" died in the AI era. On Netflix, open-ended plots multiply, single-line fairy tales recede. Audiences finally accept that life is never a straight line. At the WBSC Premier12, Taiwan's team played at the Tokyo Dome, where 70% of the crowd was Taiwanese. Did they win? Not the point. The point was: Taiwan, in that moment, gathered.

06

我們 勝過我

We Beats Me

老派敘事是英雄主義:T-800 拿著槍救世界,一夫當關萬夫莫敵。但漫威《復仇者》上映後第二週票房暴跌 78%,觀眾不再相信「一個人出現就能拯救世界」。當代敘事是集體主義。詹姆士柯麥隆從《魔鬼終結者》走到《阿凡達》,正是同一個導演意識到時代的轉向。伍佰演唱會最神奇的地方是他幾乎不唱,而是把麥克風遞給觀眾。觀眾要的不是看到他,觀眾要的是:我也是這場現場的一部分。

The old narrative was heroism: a T-800 with a gun saving the world, one man holding off ten thousand. But Marvel's Avengers films now drop 78% in their second weekend; audiences no longer believe one person can save the world. Contemporary narrative is collective. James Cameron walked from The Terminator to Avatar: the same director catching the turn of the times. The most magical thing about a Wu Bai concert is that he barely sings; he hands the mic to the audience. They didn't come to see him. They came to be part of the moment.

07

意義感 勝過好

Meaning Beats Better

過去三年台灣最暢銷的書是《原子習慣》,大家都在追求「更好」。今年最暢銷的書叫《也許你該找人聊聊》《我可能錯了》:一本是治療師寫治療,一本是漸凍症患者寫意義。整個社會的關注從「How(怎麼變得更好)」轉向「Why(為什麼要做這件事)」。以前不太談 why,因為 why 沒有標準答案。但 AI 時代你不再需要標準答案,你需要的是只有你自己能回答的東西。

Taiwan's bestseller for the past three years was Atomic Habits, everyone chasing "better." This year's bestsellers are Maybe You Should Talk to Someone and I May Be Wrong: one written by a therapist about therapy, the other by a man with ALS about meaning. Society's attention has shifted from "how (to be better)" to "why (am I doing this)." We rarely talked about "why" before, because "why" had no standard answer. But in the AI era you no longer need standard answers. You need what only you can answer.

七種轉變共享同一個底層邏輯:人類正在重新爭取「真實」的話語權。不完美、生活經驗、脆弱、現場、過程、我們、意義:這七個詞看似不同,本質上都在說同一件事:在 AI 能無限複製「乾淨完美單一英雄完整結局」的時代,人類的價值來自所有 AI 仍無法觸及的維度。粗糙、矛盾、流動、不可重複、共在、無解。看清這個底層邏輯,你就不需要記七條,你只需要不斷追問:這件事 AI 做不到的部分在哪裡?

The seven shifts share one substrate: humans are reclaiming the right to define "the real." Imperfect, lived, vulnerable, live, process, we, meaning: these seven words look different, but they all say one thing. In an era when AI can infinitely reproduce "clean / perfect / lone hero / complete ending," human value resides in dimensions AI cannot yet reach: roughness, contradiction, flow, irreproducibility, being-with, unanswered. See this substrate clearly and you don't need to memorize seven points. You only need to keep asking: what part of this thing can AI not do?

Bridge

看清了七種轉變,然後呢?

Once You See the Seven Shifts, What Then?

看見不是終點,而是行動的入口。MIT 史隆商學院的 EPOCH 框架(共情、在場、觀點、創意、希望)直接對應這七種轉變,給出一套人類在 AI 時代仍能站穩的能力地圖。再配上 Triple A 創造力(增強、適應、當責),理論就能變成可走的路。

Seeing is not the destination but the doorway to action. MIT Sloan's EPOCH framework (Empathy, Presence, Opinion, Creativity, Hope) maps directly onto these seven shifts, sketching the human capabilities that remain solid in the AI era. Combined with Triple A creativity (Augmentation, Adaptation, Accountability), the theory becomes a path you can walk.

這些應用、整合洞見、四週行動轉化、以及最重要的「迴聲預言」(讀完之後它會在哪些未來時刻突然回來找你),都收錄在這份整理的下篇〈行動之卷〉。上篇是看見,下篇是行動。

All of this (the application, the integrative insights, the four-week action plan, and most importantly the Echo Prophecy, those future moments when this talk will return to find you) lives in Scroll Two: Action. The first scroll is seeing; the second is doing.

真正的故事從來不是「你說了什麼」,而是「你活了什麼」。
The real story is never what you said but what you lived.
